Level of Risk Information

High: This is the highest level of severity for vulnerabilities. High-level vulnerabilities are often the most critical and can be exploited by malicious actors or attackers with programming skills to compromise an application or website. Examples include SQL Injection and Remote Code Execution.
Medium: Medium-level vulnerabilities are less severe than high-level ones but can still be used to launch attacks on applications or websites. The severity may increase if these vulnerabilities are exploited repeatedly. Examples include Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) at the medium level.
Low: Low-level vulnerabilities are the least severe and typically not very dangerous to an application or website. However, they should still be addressed when found. Examples include Low-level Information Disclosure.
Informational: Informational vulnerabilities have no severity and cannot be used for attacks on applications or websites. They often provide additional information about the application or system that may be useful for testing or security assessment purposes.
